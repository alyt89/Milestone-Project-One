Discover Ski Cross

Our Aim

We want to spread the word about Ski Cross and give people of all ages all across the UK the chance to experience the thrill of this unique sport.

Getting into a new sport can feel quite daunting sometimes so we are focused on not only giving people of all abilities the chance to practice Ski Cross but also providing the basic equipment needed at our events.

We are passionate about getting people involved in the sport through various dry Ski Slope facilities across the country, who knows, the 2026 Winter Olympics could well be a Ski Cross Club member!

Our History

We were set up in 2018 after the success of Team GB in the Winter Olympics sparked a huge interest in winter sports. We held our first event in London the same year where almost 100 people attended.

Since then we have held 30 events allowing people to discover this amazing sport reaching out to around 3,000 people at 5 different locations across the country.

What is Ski Cross?

Ski Cross is a winter sport that mixes different disciplines and skills you may already know from traditional skiing. Whereas in most other ski and snowboard events the competitors are either timed or scored individually, in Ski Cross 4 skiers compete at the same time to get to the finishing line first.

Ski Cross as a sport began in the 1970s in incorporates features from both downhill and freestyle skiing. While speed is most definitely of the essence there is a tactical element to the sport as you travel down the course with your competitors across jumps, rumble tracks and sharp, banked turns where gaining the optimal line and position is key.

Ski Cross first made an appearance at the 2010 Winter Olympics and has since become one of the most anticipated and watched events in recent years. If high speeds, big jumps and a keen eye for the right racing line sound like your kind of thing then Ski Cross is definitely for you!
